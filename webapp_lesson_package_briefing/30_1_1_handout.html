Key Takeaways
  • The atmosphere is 78% nitrogen, 21% oxygen
  • Water vapour (0–4%) drives all weather
  • The troposphere (to ~11 km): we fly here, weather happens here
  • ISA: 15 °C, 1013.25 hPa, lapse ~2 °C / 1,000 ft
  • Pressure halves every ~18,000 ft. Warm & humid air = less dense.
  • Tropopause: higher at equator/summer, lower at poles/winter
Gas Composition & Water Vapour
1.1.11.2.1
Dry air by volume
Dry air: Nitrogen 78%, Oxygen 21%, other gases ~1% (Argon 0.93%, CO₂ 0.04%). Constant to ~60 km. Oxygen: breathing + engine combustion.
Water vapour (0–4%) is separate from dry air. The only gas that changes state (gas ↔ liquid ↔ solid). State changes release/absorb latent heat — drives all weather. No water vapour = no clouds, no rain, no fog.
N₂ + O₂ = 99% of dry air. Everything else is trace.
Troposphere: ~75% of mass, ~99% of water vapour.
Warmer air holds more water vapour → more intense weather.
Key Relationships
1.1.4
  • ↑ Altitude → ↓ Pressure (halves every ~18,000 ft)
  • ↑ Altitude → ↓ Temperature (troposphere)
  • ↑ Altitude → ↓ Density
  • ↑ Temperature → ↓ Density (warm = less dense)
  • ↑ Humidity → ↓ Density (H₂O lighter than N₂/O₂)
Pressure = weight of air above. Higher up = less.
Density falls with altitude, heat, humidity.
Humid air is less dense than dry air.

Atmospheric Layers & Temperature
30.1.1 — LOs 1.1.2, 1.1.3, 1.2.3
Layers defined by temperature change. Troposphere (to ~11 km) — all weather. Stratosphere (11–50 km) — stable. Mesosphere (50–80 km). Thermosphere (80 km+). Boundaries = “pauses”. Temp falls at ~2 °C / 1,000 ft in troposphere. At tropopause: −56.5 °C. Constant to ~FL 650, then rises (ozone absorbs UV).

Tropopause, Definitions & Exam Focus
30.1.1 — LO 1.2.2
The boundary between troposphere and stratosphere. Height where temperature stops decreasing. Acts as a lid — stable air above traps weather below.
LocationHeight
Equator / Tropics16 – 18 km
Mid-latitudes (ISA)~11 km
Poles8 – 10 km
Height variation by latitude
Folds, breaks & jet streams
It is a boundary, not a layer. Warmer → higher. Summer → higher.
Varies with season & latitude — NOT water vapour (exam distractor).
